Why should I pay when there are free pregnancy apps?

Free apps are brilliant for tracking your pregnancy and learning general information and we'd encourage you to use them. But there's one thing an app can't do: be a midwife.

Apps can't answer your specific questions, reassure you when something feels off, remember your medical history, or give professional clinical advice about your symptoms. They provide information. MIM provides a midwife.

With MIM, you get a real, qualified professional who knows you and your pregnancy — not an algorithm.

How quickly will you respond to my messages?

Core Support: WhatsApp access is available Monday–Friday, 9am–5pm. We aim to respond same day within these hours. Messages sent outside service hours will be answered when we're next online.

Signature & Sustained Support: WhatsApp access runs seven days a week, 7am–11pm. We aim to respond within a few hours during these times, giving you consistent peace of mind throughout the week and weekend.

For true emergencies — heavy bleeding, severe pain, reduced baby movement — always contact maternity triage, NHS 111, or 999 first. MIM is here for guidance and support, not emergency care.

Is MIM expensive?

We understand it's an investment, so here's some context:

  • Private midwife care: £8,000–£12,000 for pregnancy
  • Doula support: £1,500–£3,000
  • Private midwife consultation: £80–£120 per session
  • NCT course: £300–£400 for eight sessions

MIM Core Support at £79.99/month — less than £2.70 a day — gives you an ongoing relationship with a qualified midwife, WhatsApp access during the working week, a monthly one-hour call, and daily expert guidance.

MIM Signature at £149.99/month extends that to seven-day access and two calls per month, still a fraction of private midwifery costs.

MIM Sustained Support at £1,399 covers ten full months of continuous care — equivalent to £140/month from early pregnancy through postpartum, including all antenatal education materials.

Can I just pay per session?

We don't offer pay-per-session because our model is built on continuity. A single midwife consultation elsewhere costs £80–£120. With MIM, you get ongoing support from someone who knows you, your history, and your concerns — not isolated appointments with no context.

Core and Signature have no minimum term, so you can cancel anytime. Sustained Support is a one-time payment designed for those who want certainty across the full journey.

How does MIM compare to NHS care?

NHS midwives are exceptional, and MIM will never replace them. NHS care is essential — we work alongside it, not instead of it.

The reality is that NHS midwives typically carry caseloads of 80–100 women, appointments last 15–30 minutes, and you may see a different midwife each time. That's not a criticism — it's the pressure the system is under.

MIM is for the space between appointments. When you have a question at 9pm. When something feels off and you need reassurance. When you want someone who knows your journey. Most of our clients use both — NHS for clinical care and monitoring, MIM for ongoing support and peace of mind.

How does MIM compare to hiring a doula?

Doulas provide wonderful emotional and practical support and are brilliant birth companions. Here's how they compare to MIM Sustained Support:

Doula (typically £1,500–£3,000):
Birth attendance, two to three antenatal meetings, phone support, and emotional support throughout. Note that doulas are not medically trained.

MIM Sustained Support (£1,399):
Ten months of continuous support across pregnancy and postpartum, from a qualified midwife. Regular scheduled calls, seven-day WhatsApp access, complete antenatal education, and extended postnatal support. MIM does not include birth attendance.

Similar investment, different model. Some clients choose both — and we think that's a wonderful combination.

How do the calls work?

All calls are scheduled in advance rather than on demand. This ensures you get focused, uninterrupted time with your midwife — not a rushed slot.

Core Support includes one one-hour call per month. Signature and Sustained Support include two. You can choose video or phone, and you'll receive a 24-hour reminder automatically. Calls are scheduled by mutual agreement and we're flexible around your work and family commitments.

For questions between calls, that's exactly what WhatsApp is for.

Can I cancel my subscription?

Core and Signature Support: Yes, anytime. No minimum term, no penalties.

It's designed for those who want the certainty of uninterrupted care without monthly decision-making — and we find that certainty significantly reduces anxiety for the women who choose it.

Can I upgrade or downgrade my package?

Upgrading from Core to Signature: Yes, at any time. You'll pay the difference and gain immediate access to extended hours and an additional monthly call.

Moving from Signature to Sustained: We'll discuss credit for months already paid and transition you smoothly to the complete care pathway.

Downgrading from Signature to Core: Yes, from your next billing cycle.

What if I have an emergency?

MIM is not an emergency service. If you experience heavy bleeding, severe pain, reduced or absent baby movement, severe headache with visual disturbance, signs of preterm labour, or anything that feels seriously wrong — contact maternity triage, NHS 111, or 999 immediately.

MIM is here for guidance, reassurance, education, and support between NHS appointments. Your safety always comes first.

Will I always speak to the same midwife?

Yes. You'll be matched with your dedicated midwife from the start — the same person who answers your WhatsApp messages, takes your calls, and gets to know your history, your concerns, and your journey.

That continuity is at the heart of what makes MIM different, and research consistently shows that continuity of midwifery care leads to better outcomes, lower intervention rates, and greater confidence going into birth.

What happens after I give birth?

Core and Signature Support: Your support continues through the first four weeks postpartum — often the period when you need it most. NHS midwives typically discharge at day ten, leaving a gap at a time when feeding, recovery, and adjustment questions don't stop. MIM is there to bridge that gap.

Sustained Support: Extended postnatal support is built into your package, well beyond the standard four-week period. The same continuity you had throughout pregnancy continues into the postpartum — no gaps, no handovers, no starting again with someone new.

Is everything confidential?

Absolutely. Everything you share with us is confidential, protected under GDPR, and bound by NMC (Nursing and Midwifery Council) professional standards and the midwifery code of conduct. Your privacy and trust are fundamental to everything we do.
