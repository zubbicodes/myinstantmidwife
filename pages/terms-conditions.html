1. About MIM

My Instant Midwife (MIM) is an online midwifery support service providing virtual care, guidance, and education to women and families during pregnancy and the postpartum period. MIM is operated by qualified, NMC-registered midwives and delivered entirely through digital platforms.

2. What MIM Provides

MIM subscription services include, depending on your chosen package:

  • Virtual midwifery support via WhatsApp messaging
  • Scheduled video and telephone consultations
  • Daily educational emails tailored to your stage of pregnancy
  • Downloadable antenatal education materials (included with Sustained Support or available to purchase separately)

MIM does not provide:

  • Emergency medical care or out-of-hours crisis support
  • Physical or clinical examinations
  • Diagnostic services of any kind
  • Prescription or recommendation of medications
  • Birth attendance or labour support
  • A replacement for NHS maternity care
Important: MIM is a supplementary support service designed to work alongside, not instead of, your NHS maternity care.

3. Professional Standards

All MIM midwives are registered with the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) and operate in full accordance with the NMC Code of Professional Standards. MIM maintains professional indemnity insurance and complies with all applicable GDPR and data protection legislation.

4. Client Responsibilities

By subscribing to MIM, you agree to:

  • Register with an NHS midwife or GP for your pregnancy care and attend all scheduled NHS antenatal appointments
  • Provide accurate and complete information about your medical history and pregnancy
  • Inform MIM promptly of any complications, changes in your condition, or concerns
  • Follow NHS guidance for any urgent or emergency situation
  • Not rely solely on MIM for your maternity care
🚨In an Emergency: Always contact your maternity triage unit or call 999 immediately for emergencies. Emergencies include but are not limited to:
  • Heavy bleeding
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Reduced or absent fetal movements
  • Severe headache with visual disturbances
  • Signs of premature labour
  • Any situation where you feel something is seriously wrong
  • MIM is not an emergency service and must not be used as one
